Kori Rotti

        Kori Rotti is a traditional, iconic dish from the Tuluva community in the coastal Tulunadu region (Mangaluru/Udupi) of Karnataka, India.
        It consists of a thin, crispy, dried rice wafer (rotti) served with a spicy coconut-based chicken curry (kori gassi).
        Its origins are deeply rooted in coastal agricultural traditions, designed to maximize shelf life for stored food, often served during festivals and family celebrations.

Key Historical and Cultural Aspects:

        Origin: The dish is a staple of Tuluva culture, with debate over whether it originated in Udupi or Kundapura. 
        It is often associated with the Bunt community of Mangaluru
